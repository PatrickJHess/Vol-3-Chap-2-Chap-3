Manipulating Dates#

Background#

This chapter uses the Python libraries Pandas and NumPy as well as the builtin library datetime, module calendar, and dateutil. The basic usage of Python and NumPy is detailed in two Notebooks: A Quick Introduction to Pandas and A Quick Introduction to NumPy. The notebook [A Quick Introducton To Manipulating Dates]((https://patrickjhess.github.io/Introduction-To-Python-For-Financial-Python/Manipulating_Dates.html#a-quick-introduction-to-manipulating-dates) illustrates datetime, calendar, and datetutil. These Notebooks are part of the introductory volume Background Material: An Introduction to Python for Financial Python. Portions of the volume will be cited throughout this chapter. Feel free to consult these materials as needed for greater discussion of Python concepts.

This section of Chapter Two details the application of datetime, calendar, and dateutil. Understanding date manipulation is essential for tasks such as calculating accrued interest, determining bond payment dates, and analyzing various financial instruments, including equities and derivatives.

Assign values to settlement_date and maturity_date#

The datetime library offers two distinct but related objects. For calculating accrued interest, the date object, with its year, month, and day attributes, provides sufficient detail. The datetime object includes additional attributes such as hour, second, and microsecond, that are unnecessary in the calculation of accrued interest. The more detailed attributes of the datetime object will be needed in other chapters.

The date object takes the following integer arguments:

  • year: e.g., 2025

  • month: e.g., 1 for January

  • day: e.g., 1 for the first day of the month

For example, the code below assigns January 21\(^{st}\), 2025, to the settlement date and April 30\(^{th} 2035\) to the maturity date. Both dates are then displayed using an f-string

settlement_date=date(2025,1,21)
maturity_date=date(2035,4,30)
display(f'Settlement date {settlement_date} & Maturity date {maturity_date}')

'Settlement date 2025-01-21 & Maturity date 2035-04-30'

The calendar module.#

The calendar module, frequently used for generating monthly or yearly calendars, also serves a crucial function in calculating accrued interest by identifying the last day of a month. This is relevant for bonds that mature on the last day of a month, as all payment dates will similarly fall on the last day of a month.

The monthrange() method:

  1. accepts the year and month as integer inputs;

  2. returns a tuple containing:

    • the day of the week for the first day of the month (with Monday represented as zero);

    • the last calendar day of the month;

The code below shows the results for November 2025.

# The year is 2025 and the month is 11
first,last=calendar.monthrange(2025,11)
display(f'Day Of Week for First Day {first} And Last Day Of The Month {last}')

'Day Of Week for First Day 5 And Last Day Of The Month 30'

Determine if maturity date is last day of the month#

  1. A common practice involves using an underscore (_) to disregard a value, as seen here when ignoring the day of the week for the first day of the maturity month.

  2. The variable IsLast is assigned the boolean value that results from asserting the equality of maturity_date.day to the last day of the maturity month.

_,last=calendar.monthrange(maturity_date.year,maturity_date.month)
isLast=maturity_date.day==last
display(f'Maturity date of  {maturity_date} is last day of month? {isLast}')

'Maturity date of  2035-04-30 is last day of month? True'

The relativedelta method of dateutil#

The dateutil library’s relativedelta method facilitates date and time calculations. By knowing either the last or next payment date, relativedelta can be employed to deduce the other. The method accepts arguments specifying time intervals in years, months, days, and so forth. For instance, a semi-annual bond would require assigning a value of six to the months argument.

The cell below demonstrates the use of relativedelta by adding and subtracting six months from maturity_date. It’s important to note that this operation disregards the month-end, effectively adding or subtracting six months minus one day.

# Assign delta_time the value retyrbed by relativedelta: monhs=6
delta_time=relativedelta(months=6)
# Add six months tot the maturity_ date
display(f'Six Months After Maturity Date {maturity_date+delta_time}')
# Subtract six months from the maturity_date
display(f'Six Months Before Maturity Date {maturity_date-delta_time}')

'Six Months After Maturity Date 2035-10-30'

'Six Months Before Maturity Date 2034-10-30'

Determine next and last payment dates for bond making semi-annual payments#

The following code calculates the next and last semi-annual payment dates. Adjustments to these calculations would be necessary for different payment frequencies.

  1. If the settlement_date is less than or equal to the maturity month and day in the current year:

    • The next payment date is the maturity month and day in the current year.

    • The last payment date is six months before the next payment date.

  2. If the settlement_date is greater than the maturity month and day in the current year:

    • The next payment date is six months after the maturity month and day in the current year.

    • The last payment date is the maturity month and day in the current year.

Days between dates: next payment, last payment, and settlement#

Calculating accrued interest requires determining the number of days between specific dates. A task easily achieved using date objects. The difference in days is an attribute derived from subtracting one date object from another. The following code cell calculates the number of days between the next and last coupon payment dates, and between the settlement date and the last coupon payment date.

#Calculate the difference between dates
days_between=next_payment_date-last_payment_date
days_since_last=settlement_date-last_payment_date
#Display the days attribute of the differences
display(f'Days Between Coupons {days_between.days} Days Since Last Coupon {days_since_last.days}')

'Days Between Coupons 181 Days Since Last Coupon 82'
